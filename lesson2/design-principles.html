Design Principles

Kendra Anderson

Visual Hierarchy

Coco Kelley

cocokelley.com

In this classy website, your eye is first drawn to the picture of a homey living space. The next thing I noticed was the logo, which is also very classy and eye-catching. Even the way it is slightly cut off at the top is visually appealing. Next, you may notice the subscription button at the bottom of the page. Last you may notice the navigation bar, or the free consultation button near the bottom. This sight did a good job of catching your eye with a photo, then imprinting their logo into your mind.

White Space and Clean Design

Jack Heart

jackheart.co

This website has a lot of white space. Although some might consider it too much, I think it works for the theme and style of the website. There is white space between almost everything on the page. The photos are the only thing that run together, but that draws you in even more. The site also looks clean. It has very few words, but still all the information you need on the main page of a t-shirt website. I think the centered logo and navigation bar also work with the style of the website, even though that is not the typical way of doing things.

Proximity

Oregon Museum of Science
and Industry

omsi.edu

OMSI's website is very visually appealing. It has beautiful photos and very little useless information. On this page for the Exquisite Creatures exibit, they use proximity to define different sections of the page. The information about the exibit and how to get tickets are grouped together. The navigation bar is clearly separated from the rest of the page. The photo, which is related to the exibit content is still set apart so it can draw your eye with visual hierarchy. There is a note at the bottom about membership that is close to the ticket information button, because it directly relates to ticket pricing.
